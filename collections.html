Store floor

Start with Pendleton, then slow down through the cases and shelves.

Most people start near the front with Pendleton. After that, the store opens into jewelry, beadwork, fabric, beads, bags, apparel, gifts, pottery, baskets, snacks, and the useful things people come back for.

Pendleton

Blankets, bags, towels, mugs, and apparel.

Wool goods and Pendleton pieces are one of the store's main draws.

Cases

Jewelry, silver, and beadwork.

Finished pieces, button covers, and handmade work are kept where you can take your time.

Beading supplies

Seed beads, sets, colors, and kits.

Supplies stay on the floor for people doing detailed beadwork and repair.

Fabric

Yardage, patterned cloth, and sewing materials.

Fabric and trim keep the store useful for projects, not only finished goods.

Bags and clothing

Bags, purses, clothing, and everyday pieces.

Some pieces are made for daily use: bags, apparel, kids' clothing, and accessories.

Gifts

Home goods, regional snacks, and small finds.

Gift sets, pottery, baskets, mugs, towels, snacks, and travel goods round out the stop.

Pendleton

Pendleton has been part of the store for a long time.

Pendleton is one of the first things people recognize in the store. Blankets and wool goods sit with bags, towels, mugs, glasses, apparel, and accessories.

A 2024 County 10 profile described the gift shop as Wyoming's oldest Pendleton dealer. That is part of the store's history, not just a seasonal display.

Look for Blankets, spa towels, mugs, glasses, bags, apparel, and accessories.

Cases

Jewelry, silver, beadwork, and handmade pieces.

The cases are where the store slows down. Jewelry, silver pieces, button covers, beadwork, and small handmade goods are worth looking over carefully.

Stock changes here. Pieces come and go, and the best finds are often the ones you notice after a second look.

Materials and supplies

For people who make, mend, and keep projects going.

Fabric by the yard, seed beads, ribbon, and beading supplies make this more than a gift shop. These are working materials for sewing, beadwork, repair, and projects.

Fabric Patterned cloth, yardage, and textile displays.
Beads Seed beads, color sets, and supplies for detailed work.

Gifts and everyday goods

The smaller shelves matter too.

Not everything in the store is a showpiece. People also come through for bags, kids' clothing, mugs, towels, pottery, baskets, huckleberry snacks, gift sets, and the kind of small goods they pick up while looking around.

For the road Regional snacks, small gifts, travel goods, and easy-to-carry pieces.
For home Pottery, baskets, mugs, towels, and useful goods with pattern and color.
