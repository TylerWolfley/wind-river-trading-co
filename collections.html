Collections

Specific categories people actually come here to see.

Inside the store, the mix is broad: beadwork, jewelry, artwork, clothing, shoes, Pendleton blankets and coats, leather goods, drums, gift items, and one-of-a-kind pieces.

Jewelry & Beadwork

Core categories on the floor

Jewelry and beadwork are two of the categories people most consistently associate with the trading company, and they remain part of the store’s core identity.

Artwork

Work by Northern Arapaho and Eastern Shoshone members

Artwork and handcrafted pieces by Northern Arapaho and Eastern Shoshone makers are part of what gives the store its strongest sense of place.

Pendleton

Blankets and coats with a long local history

Pendleton blankets and coats have long been one of the best-known categories in the building and one of the easiest ways to recognize the store’s Western side.

Clothing & Shoes

Wearables alongside art and gifts

The store is not limited to art and gift pieces. Clothing and shoes are part of the mix too, which is one reason the floor feels like a real trading post rather than a narrow boutique.

Leather Goods & Drums

Handmade goods that round out the store

Leather goods, drums, and smaller handcrafted items help round out the store and keep the collections from feeling repetitive.

One-of-a-kind pieces

Not just standardized gift stock

A big part of the draw is that the store still leaves room for distinctive pieces, not just standardized travel merchandise.

What shapes the mix

The store is broad because it serves locals and visitors at the same time.

This is not a narrow gallery shop. The store serves local customers year-round and also catches the summer route headed toward Yellowstone, so the shelves are broader than a simple visitor shop.

The trading company also works to carry as much locally made work as it can. The result is a mix of Native and non-Native merchandise with a strong local-maker presence rather than a generic travel-center assortment.
