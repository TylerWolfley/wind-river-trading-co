Main Floor Categories

The store reads best as a few strong categories, not one vague gift mix.

The easiest way to read the floor is in four broad buckets: jewelry and beadwork, Pendleton and textiles, leatherwork and bags, and the broader shelf mix.

Jewelry / Beadwork

Case pieces and beadwork anchor the close-up browse

Jewelry, beadwork, and smaller handmade pieces give the store one of its strongest close-range categories without narrowing the stop to one counter.

Pendleton / Textiles

Textiles run from fabric and ribbon to Pendleton and apparel

Pendleton blankets and apparel are part of the mix, but so are fabric and ribbon. Together they make the textile side feel broad instead of repetitive.

Leatherwork / Bags

Carry goods and leatherwork keep the floor practical as well as visual

Bags, leatherwork, and other carry pieces broaden the store beyond shelves and blanket racks. They are part of what gives the place its trading-post feel.

Mixed Merchandise / Shelf Browse

Foods, gifts, and shelf goods keep the browse broad

Huckleberry foods, candles, soaps, toys, puzzles, pottery, and smaller shelf goods round out the broader mix, so the store never reads like only apparel or only jewelry.

Beyond The Shelves

Pottery, rugs, drums, art, and artifact material are part of the broader stop.

The store side does not stop at shelf goods and apparel. Pottery, rugs, drums, art, and artifact material are also part of what gives the property more depth.

That broader mix is one reason the collections page works best alongside the Gallery of the Wind & Museum. The same stop carries both the browseable store floor and the museum side of the property.
