How to browse

The shelves fall into a few clear groups.

These are not rigid aisles. They are the clearest way to understand the store's mix of supplies, textiles, leather goods, food, and gifts.

Cases and beadwork

Jewelry, beadwork, and handmade pieces

Display cases and shelves hold jewelry, beadwork, and other handmade items.

Textiles and Pendleton

Fabric, ribbon, blankets, and clothing

Fabric and ribbon sit alongside Pendleton blankets, clothing, and other textile goods.

Leatherwork and bags

Bags and leather goods

Bags, leatherwork, and other carry goods add another practical category.

Food and gifts

Huckleberry foods and small gifts

You will also find huckleberry foods, candles, soaps, toys, puzzles, pottery, and other gifts.

Display pieces

Not everything is shelf stock.

Artwork, rugs, drums, pottery, pipes, and artifacts give the browsing experience more depth than a typical gift shelf.

The Gallery of the Wind & Museum continues that side of the experience in a fuller museum setting.
