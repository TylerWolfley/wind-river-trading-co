How to browse

A store floor built in layers.

Use this as a practical map of what the store carries, where the floor has depth, and when to shop online or call before driving over for something specific.

Wool and apparel

Pendleton anchors the floor.

Blankets, towels, apparel, bags, mugs, and accessories fill a major part of the store.

Maker cases

Finished work stays close.

Jewelry, beadwork, silver pieces, and one-of-a-kind work are kept in display cases.

Materials

Supplies are part of the identity.

Fabric, seed beads, ribbon, and beading supplies make the store useful to makers.

Gifts and goods

The rest is built for browsing.

Kids' clothing, snacks, bags, pottery, baskets, and small goods round out the stop.

Pendleton

Blankets, apparel, bags, towels, mugs, and more.

Pendleton is one of the first things people recognize when they walk the store. Blankets and wool goods sit alongside bags, towels, mugs, apparel, and accessories.

A 2024 County 10 profile described the gift shop as Wyoming's oldest Pendleton dealer, which makes this section more than a seasonal display.

Look for Blankets, spa towels, mugs, glasses, bags, apparel, and accessories.

Finished work

Jewelry, beadwork, silver, and handmade pieces.

The cases are where the store slows down. Finished beadwork, silver jewelry, button covers, small art pieces, and other handmade work give the collections page its most personal layer.

This is also where stock changes the most. Pieces come and go, and the best finds are often the ones you notice by looking carefully through the cases.

Fabric by the yard, seed beads, ribbon, and beading supplies give this page a different purpose than a gift-shop list. These are working materials for sewing, beadwork, repair, and projects.
