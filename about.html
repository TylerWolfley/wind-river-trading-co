1946 roots

The present store grew out of the original Hines General Store.

Its roots reach back to Hines General Store, serving the Wind River Reservation since 1946. A major 1978 buildout created today's Wind River Trading Company beside the general store.

That long run is the point. The place carries trading post credibility because it has earned it over time, not because it borrows the look.

Still in use

Locals use it. Travelers know it.

This is not a themed version of a trading post. It is a working store with Pendleton, locally made beadwork and jewelry, fabric, gifts, beading supplies, and the museum side of the building all under one roof.

That range matters because people come here for real reasons, not just a look around.
