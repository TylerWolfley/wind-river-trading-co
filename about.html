The room

Useful goods, not just souvenirs.

Wind River Trading Company is not only a gift shop. Clothing and blankets, beading supplies, jewelry cases, food items, gifts, and artwork all sit within a few steps of each other.

The identity comes from that mix: practical enough for supplies and varied enough for people who want to take their time.

Supplies Beads, fabric, ribbon, and other materials make the store useful, not just decorative.
Clothing and goods Jewelry, leatherwork, Pendleton, clothing, and bags give the browse real range.
Food and gifts Huckleberry foods, candles, soaps, toys, puzzles, and smaller gifts offer easy take-home choices.

Why it holds together

The mix is the point.

Materials, clothing, gifts, cases, and gallery pieces all sit close enough to feel like one place, not separate attractions.

Background

A longer local thread.

County 10's 2024 history of Hines General Store connects the present-day Wind River Trading Company to a store and trading post that has served the Wind River Reservation since 1946. The story also notes that a 1978 construction project helped create the current Wind River Trading Company.

The Wind River Visitors Council lists Wind River Trading Company with the Gallery of the Wind & Museum and describes the business as a place for Native American arts, crafts, and clothing.
