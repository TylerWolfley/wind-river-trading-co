About Wind River Trading Company

A long-running trading post and gift shop.

Since 1946, Wind River Trading Company has been a place to browse supplies, clothing, gifts, food items, and the Gallery of the Wind & Museum in one stop.

What Wind River Trading Company Is

A full store, not a small checkout counter.

The store is built for browsing, with clothing, blankets, display cases, gifts, and beading supplies sharing the room.

The mix is practical and giftable: beads, fabric, ribbon, jewelry, leatherwork, Pendleton, clothing, pottery, huckleberry foods, candles, soaps, toys, puzzles, artwork, rugs, drums, and artifacts.

Supplies Beads, fabric, ribbon, and other materials make the store useful as well as browseable.
Clothing and goods Jewelry, leatherwork, Pendleton, clothing, and bags give the shelves a wide range.
Food and gifts Huckleberry foods, candles, soaps, toys, puzzles, and smaller gifts round out the stop.

Store Identity

A trading post with a broad mix.

Wind River Trading Company is part supply shop, part gift shop, and part gallery stop.

Browse Display cases, blankets, shelves, and artwork give people a reason to slow down and look around.
Supplies Beads, fabric, ribbon, and leather goods keep the store useful for makers and visitors.
Gallery The Gallery of the Wind & Museum adds artwork and artifacts to the visit.
