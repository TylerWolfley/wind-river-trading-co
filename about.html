History

Rooted in Fort Washakie

Wind River Trading Company grew out of Hines General Store, which has served the Wind River Reservation since 1946. A major expansion in 1978 created the present-day trading company beside the original store, and the business has remained in the Hines family ever since. Today it is still a working part of Fort Washakie, with deep local roots and a long place in the life of the community.

Still open

This is still a working trading post.

People still come in here for Pendleton, finished beadwork and jewelry, fabric by the yard, gifts, and beading supplies.

The gallery and museum remain part of the building.
