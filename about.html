Store History

The present store took shape beside Hines General Store.

Its roots go back to Hines General Store, serving the Wind River Reservation since 1946. A major 1978 buildout created today's Wind River Trading Company beside the original store.

The business has stayed with the Hines family across generations, which is part of why the place still feels like it belongs where it stands.

Continuity

A few facts explain why the store feels established.

This is not history pasted on afterward. The business has been carried forward in place, through family continuity, and through the kind of inventory people still come in needing.

Still In Use

It is still a working trading post, not a preserved version of one.

People come here for Pendleton, locally made beadwork and jewelry, fabric by the yard, gifts, and beading supplies. The everyday usefulness of the store is part of its identity.

The Gallery of the Wind & Museum adds a quieter museum side without separating the building from its life as a store.

Useful shelves Materials, finished work, apparel, and gifts all move through the same floor.
Local relevance Locally made items and one-of-a-kind pieces still hold their place here.
Deeper visit The gallery gives the building room for artwork and artifacts connected to Shoshone and Arapaho culture.

Read The Place

The store makes the most sense when you see both sides of it.

One side is a working trading post. The other is the Gallery of the Wind & Museum. Together they explain the store's character better than either one alone.
