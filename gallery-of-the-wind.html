Inside the trading post

A compact museum room with local weight.

Gallery of the Wind & Museum sits inside Wind River Trading Company, but it has its own rhythm. It is a close-up room of Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho artwork, artifacts, photographs, handmade pieces, and historic display material.

The room feels less like a polished institutional gallery and more like a place assembled through memory: display cases, wall pieces, beadwork, old tools, clothing, painted work, and personal objects all sharing space.

Where it is Inside the trading post at 14631 Hwy. 287 in Fort Washakie.
What it holds Artwork, artifacts, photographs, handmade work, and historic displays.
How to see it Slowly. The details are the point.

Display highlights

Stories on the wall and character in the corners.

The room moves between museum, memory room, and trading-post cabinet of curiosities.

Chief Washakie

The Crowheart Butte story.

One wall display centers on Chief Washakie, the Eastern Shoshone leader whose name is carried by Fort Washakie. The panel presents the 1866 Crowheart Butte story, where Washakie and Crow leader Big Robber are remembered in a single-combat account tied to hunting grounds in the Wind River Range.

It gives the room a specific anchor: this is not just "Western" decor. It is a gallery inside Fort Washakie, speaking from the history and place around it.

Trading-post character

The odd details matter too.

Not every memorable object is solemn. The Wile E. Coyote figure, dressed in beadwork-style clothing and moccasins, sits near old gear and a tobacco tin. It brings a little humor into a room that also carries serious historical material.

That mix is part of the personality of the place. The gallery is useful because it lets visitors look closely at both cultural display work and the strange, specific objects that collect around a long-running trading post.

What to look for

A room built from objects, not just labels.

Look for beadwork, moccasins, handmade art, historic photographs, tools, pipes, pottery, painted pieces, and display cases that connect the museum room back to the store around it.

In the room

Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho artwork, artifacts, and display pieces.

Visitor listings describe the gallery as a collection of artwork and artifacts of the Shoshone and Arapaho people. On the ground, it reads as a layered room of art, history, and trading-post material.

Handmade work Beadwork, moccasins, jewelry, painted pieces, and case displays.
Historic material Photographs, newspaper-style panels, older tools, and object groupings.
Place memory Objects that make sense here because the gallery is inside the trading post.

Plan the stop

Give the gallery its own time.

The store and the gallery share a building, but the gallery deserves its own pause. Walk the room before or after shopping, then use the collections page for the retail side of the trading post.
