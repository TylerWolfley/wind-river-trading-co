Gallery of the Wind & Museum

Artifacts and artwork of the Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho tribes.

The Gallery of the Wind & Museum brings Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho artifacts and artwork into the same stop as Wind River Trading Company at 14631 Hwy. 287 in Fort Washakie.

Museum Eastern Shoshone Northern Arapaho

What makes it distinct

The museum is part of why this stop feels deeper than a standard roadside shop.

The gallery is not a side note to the store. It is one of the reasons the stop matters, because it brings artwork, artifacts, and history into the same visit instead of separating shopping from context.

Collection

Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho artwork

The collection centers Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho artifacts and artwork, which gives the room a clear cultural focus and keeps it grounded in the place where you are standing.

Location

Inside the trading company complex

The gallery shares the same Highway 287 address and phone number as Wind River Trading Company, making it easy to include in the same stop.

Context

Part of a larger reservation story

The gallery makes the most sense when it is seen as one part of a larger Fort Washakie and Wind River Reservation story rather than a stand-alone museum room.

How to approach the gallery

Use it as one part of a respectful visit to Fort Washakie and the reservation.

The Wind River Reservation is a living place, not a staged attraction. The gallery makes more sense when it is approached in that spirit, as one stop within a real community and a broader cultural landscape.

If you want to build out the visit, the Eastern Shoshone Tribal Cultural Center and Sacajawea’s Gravesite both add context to what you are seeing at the trading company and in the gallery.

Eastern Shoshone Tribal Cultural Center Located at 90 Ethete Road, with artifacts, artwork, photographs, and treaties tied to Eastern Shoshone history.
Sacajawea’s Gravesite About three miles west of Fort Washakie at the foot of the Wind River Mountains.
Wind River Reservation Trail The broader reservation trail connects important places across wide-open country and helps frame the gallery in its larger landscape.
