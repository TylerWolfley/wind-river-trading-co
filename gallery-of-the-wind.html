Gallery of the Wind & Museum

A gallery and museum stop at Wind River Trading Company.

Public tourism listings describe the Gallery of the Wind & Museum as a collection of artifacts and artwork of the Shoshone and Arapaho people. It sits at 14631 Hwy. 287 in Fort Washakie and uses the same store phone as Wind River Trading Company.

14631 Hwy. 287 (307) 332-3267 At Wind River Trading Company

What The Gallery Is

Presented publicly as the Gallery of the Wind & Museum at the trading company address.

Public reservation and tourism listings place the gallery at Wind River Trading Company on Highway 287 in Fort Washakie. It is presented as part of the same stop, not as a separate museum somewhere else in town.

Those same public listings describe it as a collection of artifacts and artwork of the Shoshone and Arapaho people. Reservation-specific descriptions also name the Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho tribes in describing the gallery.

Why The Gallery Matters To The Stop

The museum side gives the trading company a clearer cultural and artifact focus.

Regional travel references already point people to Wind River Trading Company for Native American arts, crafts, clothing, beadwork, leather goods, drums, rugs, pottery, and artifacts. The gallery gives that stop a clearer museum side.
