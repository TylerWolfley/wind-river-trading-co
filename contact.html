Nearby Fort Washakie stops

Three places that add context if you have time for more than one stop.

If you want a fuller day in Fort Washakie, these are three of the nearby places that fit naturally with a trading company and gallery stop.

Sacajawea’s Gravesite

A major historical site west of town

Sacajawea’s gravesite sits about three miles west of Fort Washakie at the foot of the Wind River Mountains and is one of the area’s best-known stops.

Eastern Shoshone Tribal Cultural Center

Artifacts, artwork, photographs, and treaties

The cultural center at 90 Ethete Road adds more Eastern Shoshone history through artifacts, artwork, photographs, and treaty material.

Reservation trail

A broader route through the landscape

The larger reservation trail runs through important places and wide-open country, which helps put a stop in Fort Washakie into a wider landscape.
