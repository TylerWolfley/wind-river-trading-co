Why Stop

More than a quick roadside gift shop.

People stop here for different reasons: Pendleton, fabric, beadwork, supplies, gifts, the gallery, or simply because a place with this kind of history is worth seeing in person.

If you have time for one meaningful stop in Fort Washakie, this is the kind that gives you both a working store and something quieter to walk through.

Since 1946 The business has served the Wind River Reservation for generations.
Pendleton Pendleton has a long place here, with the gift shop known as Wyoming's oldest dealer.
Gallery The gallery shares the building with the trading post, giving the stop more range than a single room.

Before You Head Over

A little planning goes a long way.

This is a real store, so the best way to confirm something specific is still a phone call. Facebook is useful for another current point of contact, and the gallery is easiest to appreciate when you make time for both sides of the building.

Need current stock? Call the store before you drive over for something specific.
Checking updates? Facebook is a good place to look before you head out.
Want the fuller visit? Gallery of the Wind and the collections together give the best picture of the place.
