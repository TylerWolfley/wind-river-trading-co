Why This Place Matters

A real trading post with uncommon depth.

The store is useful in the everyday sense, but it also carries history, family continuity, and a gallery that makes the stop feel more meaningful than a quick run for gifts.

History

Its roots reach back to Hines General Store.

Wind River Trading Company grew out of Hines General Store and has stayed tied to Fort Washakie and the Wind River Reservation since 1946.

Pendleton

Pendleton has held a long place here.

The gift shop is Wyoming's oldest Pendleton dealer, which gives the store lasting regional weight beyond a single category on the shelves.

Gallery

The gallery changes what the building feels like.

Artwork and artifacts connected to Shoshone and Arapaho culture sit beside the trading post, giving the building a quieter museum side.

Fort Washakie

The sense of place comes from continuity.

The store feels rooted because it is: still part of Fort Washakie, still useful to local shoppers, and still worth seeking out as a traveler.

The Trading Post Side

What people actually come in for.

Some people come for Pendleton. Some need supplies. Others are looking for finished beadwork, jewelry, fabric, or a gift that feels tied to the place.

Finished work, materials, and Pendleton each have their own place.

The shelves make an important distinction: locally made beadwork and jewelry live apart from the materials and supplies used to make new work.
