Why People Stop Here

More than a gift shop on the highway.

People stop here for beads and fabric, jewelry, leatherwork, Pendleton blankets, apparel, huckleberry foods, and the museum side of the property. It is easy to find on US 287 and worth more than a quick glance from the road.

Beads / Fabric / Jewelry

Real beading supplies and close-up case pieces

The floor mix includes finished jewelry and beadwork, but it also goes deeper into bead kits, loose supplies, and fabric goods than a generic highway stop.

Pendleton / Apparel / Leatherwork

Blankets, hoodies, bags, and Western staples

Pendleton blankets, apparel, leatherwork, and carry pieces give the store its strongest Western feel without flattening the rest of the merchandise around them.

Foods / Museum / Highway Stop

Huckleberry goods and artifact depth in one stop

Huckleberry shelf goods give the store one more practical category, and the museum artifacts on site make the stop feel deeper than a standard roadside browse.

Inside The Stop

The visit gets stronger once you step past the front porch.

The storefront brings people in, but the real draw is the combination of a wide sales floor, museum detail, and handmade merchandise in the same stop.

A Long-Running Stop

Serving Fort Washakie since 1946.

The short version belongs here: the business dates to 1946, and the present-day trading company building took shape in 1978. The fuller family and business history lives on the About page.
