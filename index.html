Why People Stop Here

More than a gift shop on the highway.

This is a working trading company with Native art, jewelry, clothing, Pendleton, leather goods, and one-of-a-kind pieces, plus a museum on site. It is easy to find on US 287 and worth more than a quick glance from the road.

Trading Company

The floor mix runs from beadwork and artwork to clothing, shoes, fabric, Pendleton, and smaller gift pieces that make the store easy to browse for a long time.

Gallery of the Wind

The Gallery of the Wind brings Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho artifacts and artwork into the same visit, so the stop carries more depth than a standard roadside store.

A Long-Running Stop

The business began here in 1946, and the current trading company took shape in 1978.

Paul and Marjorie “Peg” Hines bought the original store in 1946 after World War II. What began as one store eventually grew into Hines General Store and, a few yards to the north, Wind River Trading Company.

The major 1978 construction project created the present-day trading company building, and Dave and Ben Hines took over operations in 2004 after years of working in the family business.

1946 The business began serving the Wind River Reservation in the immediate post-war period.
1978 The construction project that opened in September 1978 created the present-day trading company.
2004 Dave and Ben Hines became the operating generation after years in the store and gift shop.

What You Will Find Inside

The store is broad, but it still feels rooted in place.

Wind River Trading Company is not built around one narrow category. The mix is part of the appeal: locally made work, Pendleton, clothing, leather goods, gift pieces, and the museum all under one roof.

Local Makers

Beadwork, artwork, and one-of-a-kind pieces

Locally made work is part of what gives the store its character, especially pieces tied to the Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho communities.

Pendleton

Blankets, coats, and familiar Western staples

Pendleton remains one of the best-known categories in the building and one of the reasons many travelers remember the stop years later.

Gallery & Museum

Artifacts that slow the visit down

The Gallery of the Wind changes the pace of the stop by putting artifacts and displays alongside the shopping side of the business.
