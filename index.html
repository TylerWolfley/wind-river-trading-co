What You Will Find Inside

A real trading company floor, not a small gift counter.

Once you step inside, the mix broadens fast: beading supplies, fabric and ribbon, jewelry, leatherwork, Pendleton, apparel, pottery, huckleberry foods, candles, soaps, toys, puzzles, and other shelves worth browsing.

Store And Gallery Together

The trading company and the Gallery of the Wind & Museum share one Fort Washakie stop.

The gallery and trading company use the same address and store phone, so visitors can browse merchandise and then step into the museum side without making a second stop.

Public tourism listings describe the gallery as a collection of artifacts and artwork of the Shoshone and Arapaho people, which is part of what makes this property feel deeper than a standard highway store.
